What was the
Cuban Missle Crisis?

The Cuban Missle Crisis occurred over a period of thirteen days between October 16, 1962 and October 28, 1962.  During the Cold war, John Fitzgerald Kennedy (JFK) placed nuclear warheads in Turkey, which was located next to the Soviet Union.  When the Soviet Union found out, they took imeadiate action.  They started to build and place nuclear Warheads in Cuba right next to the United States (U.S.).   Cuba struck a deal with Soviet Union after an event called the Bay of Pigs.  The "Bay Of Pigs" was a failed attempt by the CIA (Central Intelligence Agency) to overthrow the new small comunist country lead by Cuban nationalist Fidel Castro.  The CIA  trained 1,400 cubans who fled their homes after Castro took control over Cuba. 

     The CIA planned a full scale invasion of the island, but the rebels were outnumbered by Castro's troops.  The rebels were defeated and surrendered after 24 hours of fighting.  After this, Castro talked to soviet leader Khrushchev for help to discourage any other U.S. interference.  Khrushchev then started to place Missles in Cuba.  JFK and his advisors had a discussion and they voted in favor of retaliating, but Kennedy chose a more peaceful approach.   He chose to initiate a naval blockade.
 Soviet Union leader Nikita Khrushchev chose to send JFK a letter stating that interfering with the Soviet ships was an act of war.  John F. Kennedy responded by stating that he wasn't blocking resources and goods to Cuba, only shipments involving the construction of the missles. JFK stated in his letter it was a "quarantine" he later stated that he called it a quarantine because it sounded less hostile. During this time the alert system used by the armed forces the went to the highest that it has ever been (defcon 2).

        While the U.S ships where positioned in the Naval blockade, four submarines were positioned under them.  One Soviet submarine commander saved the world by preventing a nuclear warhead from being launched at the U.S ships, his name was Vasili Arkhipov.  After the incident, the two leaders thought it had gone far enough, and president Kennedy talked with an advisor from Mosscow.  They made an agreement to take Soviet missles out of Cuba if Kennedy took American missles out of Turkey.
